## 2. Purpose and Review Boundary

This review determines whether each proposed Amendment 001 change is authorized, semantically adequate, constitutionally coherent, stable for future layers, and ready for Founder approval of its exact wording. Founder authorization establishes that a change may be proposed; it does not establish that the proposed wording is correct.

The review does not modify the Amendment, Constitution, Founder Disposition, Issue Register, or application. It does not approve wording on behalf of the Founder, incorporate the Amendment, close an issue, ratify the Constitution, select another option, or define an implementation.

## 3. Independence Method

The review followed the required order:

1. The Current Constitution, Founder Decision Statements, Authorized Input Manifest, Issue IDs, and proposed wording were read first.
2. An initial interpretation and adversarial concern list was recorded for each change without using the amendment author's self-validation, earlier PASS reports, or detailed triage reasoning as proof.
3. The Issue Register was then consulted to reconstruct the original ambiguity and boundary.
4. The Issue Triage was consulted last to verify selected-option meaning, exact downstream ownership, and the authorized minimum change.
5. The Amendment's own scope and protection claims were treated as assertions under review, not independent evidence.
