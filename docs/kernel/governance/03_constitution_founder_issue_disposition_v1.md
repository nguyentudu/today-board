## 2. Purpose

This document is the formal location for Founder decisions on the three validated constitutional issues. It records whether constitutional change is authorized or denied, identifies any selected resolution option, defines the permitted amendment scope, preserves constitutional and downstream ownership boundaries, and creates traceability from issue to decision to any future amendment.

**This document records constitutional decisions.**

**It does not amend the Constitution.**

**It does not ratify the Constitution.**

**It does not authorize implementation.**

The Founder Approval Record dated 15 July 2026 explicitly accepts all three validated issues for Amendment 001, limited to their Constitution-owned portions. It also authorizes selection of the uniquely recommended triage option for each issue, requires operational portions to remain with named downstream owners, and conditions authorization on complete issue-specific scope and validation. This document records those atomic decisions; it does not draft Amendment 001.

## 3. Decision Integrity Principles

### 3.1 Explicit Authority

No recommendation becomes a decision without an explicit Founder disposition. The triage recommendation `ACCEPT FOR AMENDMENT` is decision support, not approval.

### 3.2 Atomic Decisions

Each registered issue requires its own disposition. Acceptance, rejection, deferral, clarification, or merger of one issue does not imply the same disposition for another.

### 3.3 Minimal Amendment Authority

`ACCEPT FOR AMENDMENT` authorizes only the constitutional scope explicitly recorded by the Founder for that issue. An accepted issue with incomplete scope remains incomplete and does not grant full amendment authority.

### 3.4 Downstream Separation

The Constitution may establish meaning, authority, precedence, trust boundaries, and mandatory deferrals. Language, Ontology, Canonical Model, Lifecycle, Primitive Specifications, Acceptance Specification, and Architecture retain their named operational responsibilities.

### 3.5 No Silent Expansion

Amendment 001 may not add cleanup, restructure the Constitution, introduce a Primitive, settle unrelated deferred questions, or define implementation merely because those changes appear related or useful.

### 3.6 Explicit Trust Decisions

Consent, Fresh Start, Present Only, No Old Pull, forgetting, persistence, retention, retrieval, presentation, inference, reopening, cross-session continuity, deletion, and user authority require explicit Founder language where constitutional precedence is selected. Codex must not infer a trust philosophy from a recommendation.

#### Available Resolution Options

**Option A - Continuity-claim threshold**

- **Constitutional meaning:** A context qualifies when preserving its identity or meaning across a meaningful separation may be necessary for understanding, resumption, attention, commitment, or honest disposition.
- **Advantages:** Fits the existing thesis, remains domain-independent, and supplies a semantic lower boundary.
- **Risks:** “Meaningful separation” still requires downstream examples and could remain broad in edge cases.
- **Downstream consequences:** Continuity Language defines terms; Situation Specification and Acceptance define examples and non-examples.
- **Required amendment scope:** One definition clarification and one explicit downstream deferral.

**Option B - Person-authorized return threshold**

- **Constitutional meaning:** A context qualifies when a person has authorized or expressed that it may need future continuity.
- **Advantages:** Establishes a stronger agency boundary and narrower qualification.
- **Risks:** May exclude continuity needs before explicit capture or authorization and requires later relational/imported-context rules.
- **Downstream consequences:** Consent and authorship become central to Ontology and Primitive Specifications.
- **Required amendment scope:** One qualification sentence and one authority boundary.

#### Available Resolution Options

**Option A - Authorization-bounded continuity**

- **Constitutional meaning:** Identity and preservation duties apply only within continuity the person has authorized; authorized boundaries may limit retention, retrieval, presentation, or cross-session continuity without constituting Continuity failure.
- **Advantages:** Gives consent and dignity direct precedence and prevents coercive interpretation.
- **Risks:** Requires careful downstream separation of permissions and honest treatment of technically unavoidable retention.
- **Downstream consequences:** Invariants and Lifecycle define effects; Architecture implements operation-specific permissions and truthful deletion limits.
- **Required amendment scope:** One precedence clause and one explicit operational deferral.

**Option B - Layered permission independence**

- **Constitutional meaning:** Situation identity may remain preserved while retention, retrieval, presentation, reopening, and deletion permissions are constitutionally distinct and individually controlled; Fresh Start does not itself imply deletion unless specified.
- **Advantages:** Avoids conflating hiding, forgetting, and deletion and supports precise, reversible choices.
- **Risks:** Persistent identity may still feel coercive unless default authority and No Old Pull behavior are explicit; downstream policy becomes more complex.
- **Downstream consequences:** Invariants define non-inference among permissions; Canonical Model and Architecture represent and enforce them.
- **Required amendment scope:** One separation rule, one authority rule, and one explicit downstream deferral.

**Option C - Minimum authority rule with downstream permission model**

- **Constitutional meaning:** A person's explicit continuity boundary must be honored, but the Constitution does not decide whether a named preference affects retention, retrieval, presentation, or deletion; each preference must be defined downstream before use.
- **Advantages:** Uses the smallest constitutional footprint and avoids premature operational policy.
- **Risks:** May leave precedence unresolved unless the amendment also forbids identity and preservation from overriding an explicit boundary.
- **Downstream consequences:** Invariants and Acceptance become blockers before a preference can be implemented.
- **Required amendment scope:** One must-honor rule and one strict deferral.
